Introduction

In this project, my goal is to read data from a MPU-6050 sensor, apply my . I'm trying to determine how accurate are these sensors are (read: how good of an algorithm can I make). I've chosen to go for an Arduino Uno R3 SMD and the very popular IMU: MPU-6050. My MPU is attached to a beautiful red breakout board from SparkFun (https://www.sparkfun.com/products/11028).

Soldering pin headers to breakout board

If your sensor is on a breakout board without pins, you'll have to solder on some pin headers. This is a much simpler task than you might think, so all you need is a soldering iron (your university has one) and some solder. Very simple to apply: 315 degrees celcius + 2 seconds to heat up + add solder + 1 second to sink into the hole. Don't read 100 tutorials; learn by doing (but perhaps you begin soldering your 1$ board instead of a 50$ one). If you have no idea what you are doing, then I'm just like you. One question you may have is; Which components/cables do i plug in first? Thats actually way wrong, plug them all in and then put the power on. If GND is not connected, will my breakout board fry? **** WILL IT ? **** I had to google that one myself as i wrote this. Get a bunch of jumper cables, male to male is great for breadboard connections. And learn to dissmantle a cable with a cutting (what tool) tool. You'll see that one end is not as sharp as the other, use that one. And you'll need some cable to practice on. Use libraries, by all means, but if you are writing a thesis on it you should (must) understand the underlying functionality of it. You cant research shit you dont know anything about. Processing the data by using a complementary filter, which combines the data from the gyroscope and the aceelerometer. A simple function as: newAngle = gyroAngle*a + accelAngle*(1-a) will give you a combined angle value, and to favor one value over the other you simple change the paramterer a (a is tau/tau+dt). Graphing is easily done in the Arduino IDE (version ?? and up) using the Signal Plotter found in the Tools menu. The graph auto-scales and is very simple usage: Just write to Serial your values, separated by a blank space: Serial.print(value1); Serial.print(" "); Serial.println(value2);
